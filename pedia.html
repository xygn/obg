1. Renal

  • Clinical features and management of acute post streptococcal glomerulonephritis (x2)

    Clinical Features

    • School age children, boys more commonly (ages 3-12yrs).
    • Rapid onset puffiness around eye (more in the morning) and pedal edema.
    • Characteristically cola-colored urine.
    • Oliguria
    • Hypertension
    • In atypical cases
      • Convulsions
      • Left ventricular failure
      • Acute renal failure

    Investigations

    1. CBC : Leukocytosis, Normocytic normochromic anemia
    1. ESR : increased
    1. BUN: elevated
    1. Blood creatinine: elevated
    1. Urine analysis : RBC casts
    1. Electrolytes → Hyponatremia, hyperkalemia
    1. Proteinuria → 1+ / 2+
    1. Immunofluorescence
    1. C3 levels - decreased
    1. Evidence of strep infection → Throat swab, ASO titer increased, Anti DNase B increased

    Treatment

    1. General → Bed rest, daily weight chart, BP, urine for quantity, protein and hematuria analysis
    1. Diet → Intake of protein, sodium and potassium should be restricted till the blood urea value reduces and urine output increases
    1. Weight → In severe oliguria, patient loses about 0.5% body weight per day
    1. Antibiotics → Benzathine penicillin single dose
      1. < 20 kg → 6 lakh IU
      1. > 20 kg → 12 lakh IU
    1. Hypertension → salt and water restriction, amlodipine, nifedipine
    1. Edema / CCF → Furosemide

  • Definitions of remission, relapse, frequent relapse and steroid resistance in nephrotic
    syndrome
    • Remission → Urine albumin nil or trace (or proteinuria <4 mg/m2/hrmg/m^2/hrmg/m2/hr﻿) for 3 consecutive early morning specimens
    • Relapse → Urine albumin 3+ or 4+ (or proteinuria >40 mg/m2/hrmg/m^2/hrmg/m2/hr﻿) for 3 consecutive early morning specimens, having been in remission previously
    • Frequent relapses → Two or more relapses in initial six months or four or more relapses in any twelve months
    • Steroid dependence → Two consecutive relapses when on alternate day steroids or within 14 days of its discontinuation
    • Steroid resistance → Absence of remission despite therapy with daily prednisolone at a dose of 2 mg/kg per day for 4 weeks and alternate day for next 4 weeks

    Etiology

    • Infants
      1. Congenital heart disease
      1. Myocarditis
      1. Arrhythmias
      1. Pulmonary hypertension
    • Children
      1. Rheumatic fever, Rheumatic heart disease
      1. Congenital heart disease complicated by anemia, infection or endocarditis
      1. Systemic hypertension
      1. Myocarditis, primary myocardial disease
      1. Pulmonary hypertension

    Clinical features

    • Symptoms
      • Slow weight gain : Due to easy fatigability while feeding, and due to increased energy required for breathing
        • Uncommonly : There may be unusual weight gain due to collection of water, manifesting as facial puffiness
      • Poor feeding
      • Irritable baby
    • Signs
      • Tachypnea and tachydcardia
      • Persistent cough while lying down
      • Hoarse cry
      • Wheezing
      • Basal rales

    Treatment of CCF

    General measures

    • Restriction of activities —nursing in propped up position. Small meals
    • Treatment of fever, anemia and obesity
    • Salt restricted diet

    Specific treatment

    1. Oxygen administration —humidified 40-50%
    1. Ventilator support
    1. IV fluid should be given judiciously after assessing hydration status and presence of complications such as pulmonary edema and renal failure
    1. Furosemide 1mg/kg dose +Amiloride/Triamterene
    1. Add digoxin (Total digitalizing = 0.04 mg/kg; maintenance = 0.01 mg/kg)

    1. Treatment of underlying cause
      1. Anemia by packed cells
      1. Arrhythmias
      1. Surgical correction of cardiac defects (PDA and VSD)
      1. Myocarditis - myocardial biopsy and add immunosuppression with steroids
      1. Surgeries for cardiomyopathy

    1. Increased permeability of capillary epithelium
    1. Accumulation of leucocytes in pulmonary circulation
    1. Leucocytes release mediators such as free oxygen radicals and platelet activating factors → Injure vascular epithelium

    1. Later fibrosis occurs.
    1. Microthrombi formation → increased pulmonary vascular resistance → right to left shunting.

    • Shortness of breath
    • Fast breathing
    • Fever (if due to pneumonia)
    • Tachycardia
    • Chest pain
    • Cyanosis
